Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about epoxy floor coatings, our process, and what to expect.

How long does epoxy floor installation take?

Most residential projects (garages, basements) take 1–2 days: one day for surface preparation and primer coat, one day for the colour coat and topcoat. Larger commercial or industrial projects typically take 2–5 days depending on square footage, system complexity, and cure times. We'll give you a precise timeline during your free estimate.

How long before I can walk or drive on the floor?

Light foot traffic is typically safe after 24 hours. Vehicle traffic (cars, forklifts) should wait a full 72 hours. Full cure takes approximately 7 days, after which the coating reaches maximum hardness and chemical resistance. We'll give you specific instructions for your system during installation.

What's the difference between epoxy and polyaspartic coatings?

Epoxy is a two-component resin known for exceptional adhesion, chemical resistance, and durability. It requires adequate drying time between coats. Polyaspartic is a newer technology that cures much faster (allowing same-day recoats), is more UV-stable, and handles temperature extremes better. Many of our systems use both — epoxy as the base for superior adhesion, polyaspartic as the topcoat for UV clarity and wear resistance. We'll recommend the right combination for your space and usage.

Can epoxy be applied over an existing coating?

In most cases, no. Existing coatings must be removed before a new system can be applied. This is done during our surface preparation phase using diamond grinding, shot blasting, or scarification. Proper mechanical surface preparation is the most critical step in ensuring your new coating bonds correctly and lasts as long as it should.

Is epoxy flooring slippery when wet?

A standard gloss topcoat can be slippery when wet. We offer anti-slip additives (aluminium oxide, shark grip, or quartz broadcast) that are mixed into or broadcast over the topcoat. These significantly increase traction without affecting appearance. This is standard practice for garage floors, commercial kitchens, and any space where wet conditions are expected — and we always recommend it for those environments.

How do I maintain an epoxy floor?

Epoxy floors are very low maintenance. Regular sweeping or dust mopping removes grit that can scratch the surface over time. For cleaning, use a pH-neutral cleaner with a microfibre mop — avoid acidic or abrasive cleaners. Wipe up oil or chemical spills promptly. Avoid dragging sharp metal objects or dropping heavy tools with pointed ends. With basic care, our systems maintain their appearance for years.

Do you work on both residential and commercial projects?

Yes. EpoShield installs coating systems across all three market segments: residential (garages, basements, rec rooms), commercial (retail, restaurants, offices, parking structures), and industrial (manufacturing, warehousing, food processing, pharmaceutical facilities). Each sector has specific requirements — surface preparation, coating chemistry, slip resistance, chemical resistance — and our team has direct experience with all of them.

What does the 5-year warranty cover?

Our 5-year warranty covers delamination, peeling, and adhesion failure under normal use conditions. It does not cover physical damage from sharp impacts, improper use of chemicals not approved for the coating, or flooding and standing water events beyond the design parameters of the system. Full warranty terms are provided in writing at project completion. Contact us if you have specific coverage questions before booking.
